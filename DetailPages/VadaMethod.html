INGEREDIENTS


Urad dal - 2 cup (Make 18 to 20 vadas)
Chopped onions - 1/2 cup
Green chillies - 3 or 4
Ginger - 1 inch piece
Curry leaves - 1 sprig
Salt - to Taste
Oil - to fry


METHOD

1.   Soak urud dhal by adding enough water. The dhal has to be soaked for somewhere between 1 to 3 hrs (longer the better).

2.   Now wash the dhal.

3.   Grind urud dhal, green chillies, ginger together by adding enough water little by little. If too much water is added to the batter,
      vada will absorb lot of oil. Remember to get soft vada the technique is to grind the vada batter perfectly. The batter should
      resemble the consistency of whipped cream. Add onions and curry leaves to the vada batter.

4.   The salt is to be added to the vada batter just before when we start to make the vada. Salt added to the batter and
      left for a long time will make the batter consistency little watery.

5.   Keep oil in the kadai and heat it.

6.   Take a thick plastic sheet for the size of your palm and place it in your hand. Take a small sized batter, place in the plastic sheet
      on your hand, flatten it a little and make a whole in the centre with your finger and put it in the oil. Keep water in a bowl next to
      you and keep dipping your hands inside it everytime when you take the vada batter to make vada, so that the batter doesnâ€™t get
      stuck to your hands and it comes out from the plastic sheet in a clean way.

7.  Fry the vada thorughly by turning occasionally. Once fried remove from heat and place it on the strainer.

8.   Serve with sambar or chutney of your choice.
