INGEREDIENTS


Instant oats - 1 cup
Wheat Rava - 1/2 cup
Water - 3/4 or as required
Curd - 1/2 cup
Carrot - 1 medium sized grated
Curry leaves - few
Mustard seeds - 1/2tsp
Channa dal - 1/2tsp
Urad dal - 1/2tsp
Green chilli - 1 or 2 finely chopped
Salt - to taste
Coriander leaves - 2 tsp finely chopped
Baking soda - 2 Pinches
Oil - to grease idli plates


METHOD

1.   In a saucepan, dry roast the oats on medium heat for about 4 to 5 minutes until it gets
nicely toasted and turns slightly brown in color. Take care it does not burn by stirring it.

2.   Remove the oats to a plate and allow it to cool down. Powder it into a coarse mixture by pulsing
it a few times in a blender/mixer. Do not make it into a fine powder and Transfer to mixing bowl.

3.   Dry roast rava till nice aroma rises....it may take 3-4 mins, then set aside. Transfer to mixing bowl.

4.   Heat oil in a vessel. Add mustard seeds and as they begin to splutter, add channa dal and urad dal and
let the dal turn red. Add chopped green chilies, curry leaves and saute for a few seconds ,add grated carrot and Coriander
leaves saute for another 2 mts. turn off heat and let it Cool.

5.   Add this to the powdered oats and wheat mixture. Add yogurt, salt ,turmeric powder and required water to reach the
idli batter consistency.Add the baking soda | eno fruit salt to this.

6.   grease the idli plates.Pour this oats idli batter to this and steam it for 10 to 12 minutes.Insert a knife to check whether it is done . Let it stay 3 to 5 minutes

7.Take out from the moulds and enjoy hot. It tasted well even when it not very hot.
